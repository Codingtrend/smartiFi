Finding Your MAC Address

Your MAC Address is a unique 12-character identifier for your device's network hardware. Here’s how to find it:

On Windows:

  1. Press Win + R to open the Run dialog.
  2. Type cmd and press Enter to open the Command Prompt.
  3. In the black command window, type ipconfig /all and press Enter.
  4. Look for the "Wireless LAN adapter Wi-Fi" section and find the "Physical Address". That's your MAC Address.

On macOS:

  1. Click the Apple icon and go to System Settings (or System Preferences).
  2. Select Network from the sidebar.
  3. Select Wi-Fi from the list, then click the Details... button.
  4. The "Wi-Fi Address" listed at the bottom is your MAC Address.

On Android:

  1. Open Settings and tap on Network & internet > Internet.
  2. Tap the gear icon ⚙️ next to your connected Wi-Fi network.
  3. Scroll down to find your "Device MAC address".
